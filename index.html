UK tech is world-class, and pulling further ahead in Europe

Venture capital has rebounded, AI is now the dominant driver, and the strength runs well beyond London. The data, by Dealroom.co.

UK venture capital, by year

Annual VC raised by UK-headquartered startups, $B. 2026 is year-to-date.

After the 2021 boom and the global 2022-23 correction, UK VC has turned back up: $23.7B in 2025, with 2026 already on a stronger pace. The recovery coincides with a notably pro-tech government since mid-2024.

Source: Dealroom.co. HQ basis; VC rounds only.

UK venture capital, by quarter

Quarterly VC raised by UK startups, $B. The quarterly view shows momentum building through 2025-26.

Source: Dealroom.co.

AI is the engine

AI VC raised by UK startups ($B, bars) and AI as a share of all UK VC (%, line).

AI funding has gone from a sideshow to the main event: AI made up about a third of UK VC in 2025, and the great majority of it so far in 2026. The UK is building real depth in foundation models, AI infrastructure and applied AI.

2026 is a partial year; the AI share spikes partly because a handful of very large AI rounds land early in the year. The direction of travel, however, is unmistakable.

Source: Dealroom.co. AI = startups tagged Artificial Intelligence.

The UK's share of European VC is rising again

UK VC as a percentage of all European VC, by year.

The UK is in a league of its own in Europe: roughly four in every ten European VC dollars now go to UK startups. After dipping during the correction, the UK's share has climbed back to its highest level in years.

Source: Dealroom.co. UK and Europe on the same HQ / VC basis.

A top-3 nation for unicorns

Number of unicorns & $1B exits by country (founded-or-HQ basis). Top 10, with the UK excluding London shown for comparison.

The UK is the #3 country in the world for unicorns, behind only the US and China, and #1 in Europe by a distance. Strikingly, even if you removed London entirely, the rest of the UK would still out-rank France, Sweden and Australia. London is a world-class asset, not a crutch: the depth is national.

Source: Dealroom.co. Verified unicorns & $1B exits, founded-or-HQ.

Unicorns across the UK, beyond London

London is exceptional, and that is a feature, not a problem. Beyond it, Cambridge, Oxford, Manchester, Bristol and Edinburgh each hold their own against tier-2 hubs across Europe. The opportunity is collaborative: a thriving London and a thriving wider UK reinforce each other rather than compete.
